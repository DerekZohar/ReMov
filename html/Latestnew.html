Morfydd Clark On Being Cast As Galadriel In Amazon’s Lord Of The Rings Series

Even if she’s not a household name quite yet, Morfydd Clark is everywhere as of late – popping up in the BBC’s Dracula and His Dark Materials, Armando Iannucci’s The Personal History Of David Copperfield, and taking the lead role in Rose Glass’s startling religious psychological-horror Saint Maud. And now she’s heading into her biggest role yet, joining Amazon’s Lord Of The Rings series – actually more of a Middle-earth prequel, set a literal age prior to the story of Frodo Baggins – as ethereal Elf Galadriel, memorably played in Peter Jackson’s trilogy by Cate Blanchett.

Possessor Director Brandon Cronenberg On Inheriting A Body-Horror Legacy – Exclusive Image

There are few names bigger in the body-horror pantheon than David Cronenberg – the guy who slowly turned Jeff Goldblum into an acid-spewing mutant bug in The Fly, popped heads in Scanners, and declared ‘long live the new flesh’ in Videodrome. And speaking of new flesh, get ready for a new Cronenberg body-horror in the form of Possessor – except, this one comes not from David, but from his filmmaker son Brandon.

Chadwick Boseman – An Empire Podcast Special

In this very special edition of the Empire Podcast, Chris Hewitt, Helen O'Hara and Amon Warmann are joined by Sam Clements, host of Picturehouse Cinemas' The Love Of Cinema podcast, for an in-depth discussion of the career of the great Chadwick Boseman, whose untimely death in August left behind an indelible cinematic legacy.

The Lion King Sequel Coming From Moonlight Director Barry Jenkins

If it didn’t necessarily win everyone over on an artistic level with its highly faithful take on the 1994 animated classic, Disney’s reimagined Lion King – which presented a photorealistic ‘live-action' (if really completely computer-generated) version of the Hamlet-with-lions story – made a shedload of cash at the box office. With a worldwide haul of $1.65 billion, everything the light touched in summer 2019 more or less did belong to Jon Favreau’s new version. And now, it’s getting a sequel from an unlikely source: director Barry Jenkins, behind the Oscar-winning Moonlight and If Beale Street Could Talk.
